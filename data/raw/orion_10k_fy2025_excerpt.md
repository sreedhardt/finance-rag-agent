# Orion Semiconductor, Inc. — Annual Report (Form 10-K), Fiscal Year 2025 (Excerpt)

## Item 7. Management's Discussion and Analysis of Financial Condition and Results of Operations

### Overview of Results

Total revenue for fiscal year 2025 was $8,700 million, an increase of 31%
compared to fiscal year 2024. Growth was led by the Data Center segment, which
generated $5,585 million of revenue in fiscal 2025, up 38% year over year,
driven by sustained demand for our Orion-A3 accelerator family and expanded
hyperscaler design wins. Data Center revenue grew sequentially in every quarter
of the fiscal year, from $1,120 million in the first quarter to $1,710 million
in the fourth quarter.

The Automotive segment contributed $1,345 million of revenue, up 12% year over
year, reflecting increased content per vehicle in advanced driver-assistance
platforms. The Client segment contributed $1,770 million, up 9% year over year.

Gross margin for fiscal 2025 was 54.0%, compared to 52.1% in fiscal 2024. The
improvement was driven primarily by favorable Data Center product mix,
partially offset by higher wafer input costs under our long-term capacity
agreement with Helios Foundry.

### Liquidity and Capital Commitments

Under our amended wafer supply agreement with Helios Foundry, we are subject to
a minimum annual purchase commitment of $1,200 million for fiscal 2025, rising
to $1,450 million in fiscal 2026. Purchases under this agreement are recorded
in cost of revenue as wafers are received. We also maintain a multi-year
advanced packaging supply arrangement with Meridian Substrates; purchases under
that arrangement are not subject to a minimum commitment.

## Item 1A. Risk Factors (Excerpt)

### Supplier Concentration Risk

We rely on a limited number of third-party foundries to manufacture our
products. In fiscal 2025, Helios Foundry manufactured approximately 62% of our
wafer volume, and no qualified second source exists for our most advanced
process node. Any disruption at Helios Foundry — including capacity
constraints, yield degradation, geopolitical events affecting its fabrication
sites, or a failure to agree on commercial terms upon renewal — could
materially and adversely affect our ability to meet customer demand, our
revenue, and our gross margin. Our minimum purchase commitments could also
result in excess inventory charges if demand declines below committed volumes.

### Concentration of Revenue

Our five largest customers accounted for approximately 46% of fiscal 2025
revenue, and Data Center revenue is increasingly concentrated among a small
number of hyperscale cloud providers.

## Note 14. Income Taxes (Excerpt)

Our effective tax rate for fiscal 2025 was 16.8%, compared to the U.S. federal
statutory rate of 21%. The difference is primarily attributable to federal
research and development tax credits and the foreign-derived intangible income
(FDII) deduction, partially offset by state income taxes. See the accompanying
FY2025 Tax Provision Summary for additional detail on credits and deferred tax
positions.
