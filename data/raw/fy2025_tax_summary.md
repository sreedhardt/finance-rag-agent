# FY2025 Tax Provision Summary — Orion Semiconductor, Inc.

Prepared by the Corporate Tax function for internal review and external audit
support. Figures reconcile to Note 14 of the FY2025 Form 10-K.

## Effective Tax Rate Reconciliation

| Item | Rate impact |
|---|---|
| U.S. federal statutory rate | 21.0% |
| Federal R&D tax credits | (3.4)% |
| FDII deduction | (2.1)% |
| State income taxes, net of federal benefit | +1.6% |
| Other permanent items | (0.3)% |
| **Effective tax rate, FY2025** | **16.8%** |

## Research and Development Credits

Federal research and development tax credits recognized in fiscal 2025 totaled
**$142 million**, computed under the regular credit method. Qualified research
expenses were concentrated in accelerator architecture development and advanced
packaging co-design. Credit computations and contemporaneous documentation are
retained in the tax workpaper repository and are subject to IRS examination for
open years FY2022 through FY2025.

## Capitalized Research — Section 174

As required under Internal Revenue Code Section 174, domestic research and
experimental expenditures are capitalized and amortized over five years.
Capitalization for fiscal 2025 gave rise to a **deferred tax asset of $310
million**, which management expects to be fully realizable based on projected
future taxable income; no valuation allowance has been recorded against it.

## Uncertain Tax Positions

Gross unrecognized tax benefits were $54 million at fiscal year-end, primarily
relating to the allocation of R&D credit qualified expenses. A reduction of up
to $9 million is reasonably possible within twelve months due to the expected
closure of the FY2022 federal examination cycle.

## Open Items for FY2026 Planning

1. Assess FDII benefit sensitivity to the growing share of Data Center revenue
   delivered through foreign distributors.
2. Model the cash-tax impact of the FY2026 step-up in the Helios Foundry
   minimum purchase commitment ($1,450 million) on Section 174 capitalization.
3. Evaluate state credit opportunities for the new Austin design center.
